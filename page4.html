    • How do you manage the use of shooting games?

    Shooting games, known for their engaging gameplay and competitive elements, are popular among players of all ages. While these games can offer cognitive and social benefits, excessive or unregulated play can lead to negative outcomes, such as increased aggression, addiction, and poor academic performance. Effective management strategies can help mitigate these risks, promoting a balanced approach to gaming. Key strategies include setting time limits, monitoring content, encouraging alternative activities, and fostering open communication between players and guardians.

    Setting time limits is essential in managing gaming habits. The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) recommends that parents establish clear guidelines for screen time, suggesting no more than one to two hours per day of recreational screen use for children and adolescents (AAP, 2016). Enforcing time limits helps prevent excessive gaming, which can interfere with responsibilities such as homework, chores, and family time. Using tools such as parental control settings on gaming consoles and devices can help parents monitor and manage the time spent on shooting games.

    Monitoring the content of shooting games is another crucial aspect of management. Not all shooting games are appropriate for every age group, and the level of violence can vary significantly. The Entertainment Software Rating Board (ESRB) provides ratings that help identify the suitability of games for different age groups based on their content. Parents and guardians should review these ratings and choose games that align with the playerâ€™s age and maturity level (Gentile et al., 2014). Additionally, discussing the themes of the games, such as the difference between virtual violence and real-life consequences, can help players develop a healthy perspective on what they experience in the game.

    Encouraging alternative activities is also important in managing shooting games. Engaging in physical exercise, reading, hobbies, or spending time with friends and family can provide a balanced lifestyle and reduce the overemphasis on gaming. Encouraging players to participate in social and extracurricular activities helps build a well-rounded skill set and prevents gaming from becoming the primary source of entertainment and fulfillment (Przybylski et al., 2010).

    Open communication between parents, guardians, and players is essential in managing gaming habits effectively. Discussing gaming experiences, setting rules collaboratively, and understanding the appeal of these games can foster trust and cooperation. When players feel heard and understood, they are more likely to adhere to agreed-upon guidelines and engage in responsible gaming practices.
