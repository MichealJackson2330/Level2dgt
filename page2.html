    • What are the advantages of shooting games?

    Shooting games, including first-person shooters (FPS) and third-person shooters, are often criticized for their violent content, but research has shown that they offer various cognitive, social, and psychological benefits. One key advantage is the enhancement of cognitive skills, such as attention, spatial awareness, and decision-making abilities. Studies have found that players of shooting games often demonstrate superior visuospatial skills, which involve the ability to visualize and manipulate objects in three-dimensional space (Green & Bavelier, 2012). This skill is crucial in everyday tasks like driving, navigating, and professions such as architecture and engineering. Green and Bavelier (2012) discovered that regular players of action video games can process visual information faster and more accurately than non-gamers, effectively training the brain to be more efficient.

    Another significant benefit of shooting games is the improvement of multitasking and problem-solving skills. These games require players to manage multiple objectives simultaneously, such as monitoring enemies, strategizing moves, and managing resourcesâ€”all in real-time. Boot et al. (2008) found that gamers excel in tasks requiring divided attention and can switch between tasks more efficiently than non-gamers. This is due to the fast-paced nature of shooting games, which constantly challenge players to prioritize tasks and make quick decisions under pressure, skills that are valuable in high-stress professions like emergency response and business environments.

    Shooting games also foster social interaction and teamwork, especially in multiplayer modes where cooperation is essential. Modern games like "Call of Duty" and "Overwatch" encourage players to work in teams, communicate effectively, and coordinate strategies to achieve common goals. This teamwork aspect helps players develop leadership skills, conflict resolution, and communication abilities, which are transferable to real-world settings (Ewoldsen et al., 2012). Gaming communities also provide platforms for socialization, enabling players to connect, form friendships, and collaborate in problem-solving, enhancing social skills.

    Additionally, shooting games improve hand-eye coordination and fine motor skills due to the rapid response times and precise movements required during gameplay. Granic et al. (2014) highlighted that action video games can significantly enhance motor skills, translating to better performance in activities like sports or even surgical procedures (Rosser et al., 2007). Furthermore, shooting games can serve as stress relievers and mood enhancers, offering a break from daily stressors and an outlet for emotions. Russoniello et al. (2009) found that casual video game play led to significant stress reduction and improved mood, highlighting their role in mental health management.
