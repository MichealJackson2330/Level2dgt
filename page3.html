    • What are the disadvantages of shooting games?


    Shooting games, such as first-person shooters (FPS) and third-person shooters, are popular for their immersive gameplay and action-packed scenarios. However, despite their entertainment value, these games have been linked to various negative effects, particularly concerning cognitive, behavioral, and psychological aspects of players. One of the primary concerns is the potential increase in aggressive behavior. Studies suggest that repeated exposure to violent content in shooting games can lead to increased aggression and desensitization to violence. Anderson et al. (2010) found that violent video games, including shooters, can increase aggressive thoughts, feelings, and behaviors in both the short and long term, affecting how players react to real-world conflicts.

    Another significant disadvantage of shooting games is their potential impact on mental health, particularly in younger players. Prolonged exposure to violent games has been associated with increased anxiety, depression, and stress. Gentile et al. (2011) reported that excessive gaming could lead to addiction-like symptoms, including mood swings, social withdrawal, and a decrease in academic performance. The addictive nature of these games, fueled by rewards and constant engagement, can result in players prioritizing gaming over other important activities, such as studying, physical exercise, and social interaction, which can negatively impact overall well-being.

    Moreover, the immersive nature of shooting games can lead to a decline in academic performance and cognitive functioning due to excessive screen time and poor time management. Research has shown that children and adolescents who spend considerable time playing video games are more likely to experience reduced attention spans and difficulties concentrating on tasks unrelated to gaming (Swing et al., 2010). This reduction in focus can interfere with learning and academic achievement, as players may struggle to complete homework or pay attention during classes.

    Shooting games can also promote social isolation, as players may spend extended periods gaming alone or engaging with virtual communities at the expense of real-life interactions. The lack of face-to-face communication can hinder the development of essential social skills, making it difficult for players to build and maintain relationships outside the gaming world. This can lead to loneliness and increased feelings of alienation, particularly among young players who might substitute online interactions for genuine social connections (Kowert et al., 2015).
